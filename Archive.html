Archive

A digital file cabinet and notepad for your organizational needs.

About the project

The problem
This client saw an opportunity to join the cloud storage market but was unsure which direction they wanted to go.

The goal
With such ambiguity I needed to explore various niches within the cloud storage market and design an intuitive solution. ( This was a mock project from Bloc's immersive UX design course )

Project details

Role
UX/UI Designer - research, information architecture, branding, testing
Duration
2 months
Tools
Figma, Usability Hub, Google Suite
Deliverables
Web prototype: research, style guide, sketches, wireframes, mockups

    Usability Testing

    (Usability testing was performed with Maze.design)

    Task: Highlight the first two lines of the document.

    After performing this usability test I noticed the misclick rate was pretty high. The biggest issue was that the testers did not click the "Highlight" link first. To resolve this, I updated the layout and included a header stating the icons and text are links.
    Before After Users would not click "Highlight" on top before highlighting the first two lines. The action bar may have been too small to notice since the user is focusing solely on the document. I moved the action tools to a sidebar. The side bar is a lot more noticeable and will attract the user's eye. Additionally, I added a title giving the user an understanding what the links are.

    Conclusion

    Overall

    It took many iterations to create a better visual brand to reflect the intended personality of Archive. The usability test helped me realize that changes do not necessarily have to be major to resolve an issue. In reference to our personas, the mockups/prototype solve the organizational and tangible issues.

    What I would've done different

    I always think "The more research, the better". I would have loved to interview more students and homemakers to get a better perspective on their day-to-day lives.

    If I had more time

    I would love to explore the following:
    • Add reminders, deadlines, and recurrences for notes
    • Consider changing the color of the notes as a deadline nears
