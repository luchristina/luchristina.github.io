Cauldron

A grocery and recipe app aimed to help shoppers save time at the grocery store


View Prototype

About the project

The problem Shoppers spend more time in the grocery store than is necessary. With that wasted time, they can spend it with their loved ones! ( This was a mock project from Bloc's immersive UX design course )

The goal Cutting down shopping time will help people enjoy their saved minutes doing something else!

Project details

Role Lead UX/UI Designer - research, information architecture, interaction design, branding, testing

Duration 1 month

Tools Figma, Usability Hub, Google Suite

Research

I deployed a survey to various individuals. Of the responses I received, these were the main correlations.

  • The biggest complaints were due to time spent.
  • 73% of this sample stated that they use a physical or digital notepad.

    Brand Design

    The Brand Name

    There were many names I was considering, but "Cauldron" stood out to be the most interesting. When you think of the word "Cauldron" these descriptions come to mind:
    • A melting pot full of different items
    • Magic, mysticality
    • People creating a concoction/recipe

    Font Choice

    Since there is a lot of information on the app, the font has to be minimal. I chose is IBM Plex Sans. While it is nice and simple, there is still character to this sans-serif font.

    Logo

    Color Palette

    Many food-related apps on the market currently use the color "green" to represent freshness. I decided to use a citrus (peach, grapefruit, mandarin) palette.

    Key Findings

    Overall

    Creating a minimum viable product required many iterations. Designing the right layout was important to the usability of the app.

    What I would've done differently

    Keeping accessibility in mind from the beginning is very important and can save a lot of time during screen updates.

    If I had more time

    I would deploy this product and measure the amount of time a user saves with this app.
