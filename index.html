My path to design

The foundation- As a kid, I was fascinated by the themed layouts people were able to create on social media sites. Learning how to use Photoshop and write HTML/CSS became my fav hobby!

Getting sidetracked- Fast forward to college; I graduated with a Finance concentration and spent 4 years doing successful work (mutual funds & high-net-worth client portfolio reporting) in that field. Still, something kept reminding me of the fun I had being creative.

The start of something new- 2018 was the year I learned of UX design and redirected my career path. I engaged in a 9 month UX bootcamp where I learned the foundational ins-and-outs of the field, worked on a paid freelance project, and attended multiple UX meetups in my area. I am happy to say that I am able to utilize both my business experience as well as my creative mindset to design products that help companies improve their payment + reporting capabilities.

TLDR- I learned Photoshop, HTML, and CSS as a kid and was obsessed. Ended up getting a Finance degree, but changed my career to UX design after 4 years. I am now a full-time product designer creating scalable payment + reporting tools!
